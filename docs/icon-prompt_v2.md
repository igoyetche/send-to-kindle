# Paperboy Icon — Generation Prompt

## Prompt

Minimalist icon of a paperboy rendered in the **Vault Boy / Fallout mascot aesthetic** — 1950s atomic-age retro-futurist cartoon style, reminiscent of mid-century advertising mascots and SPECIAL-stat illustrations. Head-and-shoulders composition from a slight three-quarter angle, with one arm extended outward presenting a rolled newspaper — the classic Vault Boy "advertising gesture" of confidently displaying an item to the viewer.

**Character details:**
- Round, friendly cartoon face with a wide confident grin showing teeth.
- **Pie-cut eyes** (Vault Boy's signature) — circular eyes with a wedge-shaped slice missing from each. One eye is **winking closed** (a simple curved line), the other open and cheerful.
- Rosy, rounded cheeks — small filled circles on each side of the smile.
- A single small curl of hair peeking out from under the cap (echoing Vault Boy's distinctive forelock).
- Classic flat newsboy cap pulled low and slightly tilted — this replaces Vault Boy's usual bare head and signals "paperboy."
- Extended arm holds a rolled newspaper vertically, presenting it to the viewer the way Vault Boy presents a Nuka-Cola bottle or a weapon in promotional art. The arm is simple and tube-like with a rounded shoulder, ending in a classic cream four-fingered cartoon glove or mitten-hand.

**Style:** flat vector cartoon, bold confident outlines at consistent 3–4px stroke weight, mid-century advertising mascot energy. No gradients, no shading, no texture, no halftones, no third color. Every shape is a solid fill in one of the two palette colors. The feel is: cheerful retro mascot, 1950s print ad, Pip-Boy manual illustration.

**Palette (strict, two colors only):**
- Background and pupil/wink cutouts: navy blue `#102C54`
- Paperboy figure (outlines and fills): cream `#F5E6BE`

The entire canvas is a solid navy blue fill. The paperboy — cap, face, cap curl, arm, glove, rolled newspaper, outlines — is rendered entirely in cream. The pie-cut slice of the open eye, the curved line of the winking eye, and any tiny interior details punch through to the navy background.

**Composition:** centered on a 1024×1024 square canvas, with generous navy blue negative space around the figure so it remains legible when scaled down to 32×32 and 16×16 favicon sizes. The extended arm and rolled newspaper should fit comfortably within the canvas without crowding the edges.

**Mood:** cheerful, confident, retro, wholesome — a 1950s atomic-age mascot enthusiastically delivering today's paper. The cream-on-navy pairing feels like aged newsprint under streetlight. No text, no logo, no background scenery, no digital or tech motifs. Pure paperboy rendered as a Vault-Boy-style mascot.

**Deliverable:** flat vector-style raster, sharp edges, no anti-aliased softness. The canvas is a solid navy blue square; output the full square-filled version.

## Design Choices

| Decision | Chosen | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Palette | Navy `#102C54` + cream `#F5E6BE` | Vintage newsprint feel — aged paper on deep navy, strictly two colors |
| Style | Vault Boy / Fallout 1950s atomic-age mascot | Retro cheerful mascot aesthetic — pie-cut eyes, wink, rosy cheeks, confident presentational pose |
| Composition | Head + one extended arm presenting paper | Echoes Vault Boy's signature "advertising gesture"; preserves small-size legibility |
| Outlines | Cream (against navy background) | Cream-on-navy reads with strong contrast at all sizes; warmer than pure white |
| Metaphor | Pure paperboy, no digital cues | App context already supplies "Send to Kindle" meaning |
